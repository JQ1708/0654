Tap to Reveal Answer

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles in 2.6g of zinc. [Aᵣ: Zn = 65]

number of moles = mass⁄Mᵣ = 2.6⁄65 = 0.04 moles

Step 2: State the number of moles of hydrogen gas produced.

Element: Chlorine (Cl₂).

(ii) Copper(I) chloride contains copper ions and chloride ions. A chloride ion has the formula Cl⁻. Deduce the formula of a copper ion in CuCl. Explain your answer.

Tap to Reveal Answer

Formula: Cu⁺.

Explanation: The overall charge of the compound is neutral. Since chloride has a charge of 1-, the copper ion must have a charge of 1+ to balance the charge.

(c) Zinc displaces copper from copper sulfate. The ionic equation for this reaction is shown below. Zn(s) + Cu²⁺(aq) → Zn²⁺(aq) + Cu(s)

Tap to Reveal Answer

(aq): aqueous / dissolved in water.

(s): solid.

(ii) Describe one observation during the reaction between zinc and copper sulfate.

Tap to Reveal Answer

A pink/brown layer of copper forms on the zinc, and the blue colour of the solution fades.

(iii) Explain why this displacement reaction occurs.

Tap to Reveal Answer

This occurs because zinc is more reactive than copper.

(iv) Use the information in the ionic equation to explain, in terms of electrons, which particle is oxidised and which is reduced.

Tap to Reveal Answer

Particle oxidised: Zn (zinc atom).

Particle reduced: Cu²⁺ (copper ion).

Explanation: The zinc atom loses electrons to become a Zn²⁺ ion (oxidation), and the copper ion gains electrons to become a Cu atom (reduction).
