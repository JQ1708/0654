Question 19

(0654/43_Summer_2020_Q8) - C7. Chemical Reactions, C9. The Periodic Table, C10. Metals

Iron is a transition metal.

(a) State two properties of transition metals that are not properties of all metals.

Tap to Reveal Answer

1. They have high densities and high melting points.

2. They form coloured compounds and can act as catalysts.

(b) Describe the metallic bonding in a metal. You may draw a labelled diagram to help your answer.

Tap to Reveal Answer

Metallic bonding consists of a regular lattice of positive ions held together by electrostatic attraction to a 'sea' of delocalised electrons.

(c) Hematite contains iron oxide, Fe₂O₃. Iron is extracted from iron oxide in a blast furnace.

(i) Describe how iron is extracted from iron oxide. Do not describe how impurities are removed. You can use balanced symbol equations in your answer.

Tap to Reveal Answer

First, coke (carbon) reacts with oxygen to form carbon dioxide: C + O₂ → CO₂.

The carbon dioxide then reacts with more hot coke to form carbon monoxide: C + CO₂ → 2CO.

Finally, the iron oxide is reduced by carbon monoxide to form molten iron: Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂.

(ii) Silicon dioxide, SiO₂, is an impurity that needs to be removed from the blast furnace. The silicon dioxide reacts with calcium oxide, CaO. CaO + SiO₂ → CaSiO₃

Calculate the mass of calcium oxide needed to remove 21 kg of silicon dioxide, SiO₂. [Aᵣ: Ca, 40; O, 16; Si, 28]

Tap to Reveal Answer

Mᵣ of CaO = 40 + 16 = 56. Mᵣ of SiO₂ = 28 + (2 x 16) = 60.

From the equation, 56 g of CaO reacts with 60 g of SiO₂.

Mass of CaO = (56⁄60) x 21 kg = 19.6 kg.

(d) A blast furnace works at a very high temperature. Reactions are faster at higher temperatures. Explain why reactions are faster at higher temperatures. Use ideas about collisions between particles.

Tap to Reveal Answer

At higher temperatures, particles have higher kinetic energy and move faster. This leads to a higher frequency of collisions. Also, a greater proportion of the colliding particles will have the minimum activation energy, resulting in more successful collisions per second.

Ammonium sulfate is a fertiliser. The formula of ammonium sulfate is (NH₄)₂SO₄.

(a) Calculate the relative formula mass, Mᵣ, of ammonium sulfate. [Aᵣ: H, 1; N, 14; O, 16; S, 32]

Tap to Reveal Answer

Mᵣ = (14 + (4x1)) x 2 + 32 + (4x16) = (18 x 2) + 32 + 64 = 36 + 32 + 64 = 132.

(b) Ammonium sulfate is made by reacting ammonia with sulfuric acid. Write a balanced symbol equation for this reaction.

Tap to Reveal Answer

2NH₃ + H₂SO₄ → (NH₄)₂SO₄

(c) Potassium sulfate, K₂SO₄, is another fertiliser. In an experiment, 22.4g of potassium hydroxide, KOH, dissolved in distilled water, reacts with 19.6 g of sulfuric acid to make potassium sulfate.

(i) Calculate the number of moles of potassium hydroxide and the number of moles of sulfuric acid that react. [Aᵣ: H, 1; K, 39; O, 16; S, 32]

Tap to Reveal Answer

Mᵣ of KOH = 39 + 16 + 1 = 56. Moles of KOH = 22.4⁄56 = 0.40 moles.

Mᵣ of H₂SO₄ = (2x1) + 32 + (4x16) = 98. Moles of H₂SO₄ = 19.6⁄98 = 0.20 moles.

(ii) Using your answers from part (c)(i), deduce the balanced symbol equation for the reaction. Show your working.

Tap to Reveal Answer

The simplest whole number ratio of moles of KOH to H₂SO₄ is 0.40 : 0.20, which is 2 : 1.

Therefore, the balanced equation is: 2KOH + H₂SO₄ → K₂SO₄ + 2H₂O

Space for Fig. 2.1 graph for Haber process conditions.

Explain why in an ammonia factory, a temperature of 450 °C and 200 atmospheres pressure are used.

Tap to Reveal Answer

These conditions are a compromise. A higher temperature of 450 °C increases the rate of reaction, even though it produces a lower yield.
