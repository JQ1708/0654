(c) The student adds 0.1 g of magnesium to 40 cm³ of 0.5 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid. Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂. Show, by calculation, that the magnesium is the limiting reactant. [Aᵣ: Mg, 24]

Tap to Reveal Answer

Moles of Mg = 0.1⁄24 = 0.00417 moles.

Moles of HCl = Concentration x Volume = 0.5 x (40⁄1000) = 0.02 moles.

From the equation, the mole ratio of Mg to HCl is 1:2. Moles of HCl needed to react with all the Mg = 0.00417 x 2 = 0.00834 moles.

Since we have 0.02 moles of HCl, which is more than the 0.00834 moles needed, the HCl is in excess and the magnesium is the limiting reactant.
