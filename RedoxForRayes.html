Oxidation & Reduction

Roman numerals and oxidation numbers

Transition elements can bond in different ways by forming ions with different charges.

When naming, the charge on the ion is shown by using a Roman numeral after the element's name.

For example, iron can form different ions:

  • Iron ions with a 2+ charge, Fe2+, are called iron(II) ions.
  • Iron ions with a 3+ charge, Fe3+, are called iron(III) ions.

The Roman numeral is the oxidation number of the element.

Example: Iron Oxide

For example, iron reacts with oxygen to form iron oxide. But the name and formula of the product depend on the oxidation state of the iron ions:

Iron(II) ions

  • The iron oxide product contains iron(II) ions, Fe2+.
  • The name of this product is iron(II) oxide.
  • The formula of this product is FeO.

Iron(III) ions

  • The iron oxide product contains iron(III) ions, Fe3+.
  • The name of this product is iron(III) oxide.
  • The formula of this product is Fe2O3.

Worked Example

1. State the oxidation number of the transition metal ion in silver(I) chloride, AgCl.

2. Name, including Roman numeral, the compound with the formula CoCl2.

Show Answer

Answer 1: The oxidation number is +1 (from the Roman numeral I).

Answer 2: The compound name is cobalt(II) chloride. Since each chloride ion has a 1- charge, the cobalt ion must have a 2+ charge to balance the two chloride ions.

Oxidising & Reducing Agents

An oxidising agent is a substance which oxidises another substance during a redox reaction. Oxidising agents are themselves reduced.

A reducing agent is a substance which reduces another substance during a redox reaction. Reducing agents are themselves oxidised.

Half-Equations

These show what happens to one of the reactants during a reaction. Electrons are shown in half-equations as e-.

Oxidation (Loss of e-)

Electrons appear on the right-hand side of the equation.

Fe → Fe3+ + 3e-

Reduction (Gain of e-)

Electrons appear on the left-hand side of the equation.

O + 2e- → O2-

Redox in a Displacement Reaction

Consider the reaction between iron and copper sulfate:

iron + copper sulfate → iron(II) sulfate + copper

Fe + CuSO4 → FeSO4 + Cu

We can write this as an ionic equation:

Fe + Cu2+ + SO42- → Fe2+ + SO42- + Cu

We can then remove the spectator ions (SO42-) to see the overall change. Spectator ions are ions that are present in the reaction but do not take part in it.

Fe + Cu2+ → Fe2+ + Cu

  • The iron atom has lost electrons to become a positive ion, so has been oxidised.
  • The positive copper ion has gained electrons to become an atom, so has been reduced.

Worked Example

Which change in the following equation is oxidation?

V3+ + Fe3+ → V4+ + Fe2+

Show Answer

Step 1 - Identify the changes for each species:

  • V3+ to V4+ → V3+ has lost 1 electron
  • Fe3+ to Fe2+ → Fe3+ has gained 1 electron

Step 2 - Identify each change as either oxidation and reduction:

  • V3+ to V4+ is oxidation
  • Fe3+ to Fe2+ is reduction

Therefore, the oxidation is the change from V3+ to V4+.
